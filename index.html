I am a postdoctoral researcher in Condensed Matter Physics. My research focuses on optical properties of excitonic effects in two-dimensional transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs), including dark excitons brightening, interlayer exciton hybridizations, and exciton-polaron effects.

Education

  • Postdoc in Physics, Carnegie Mellon University, USA (2025–Present) (Advisor: Prof. Su-Fei Shi)
  • Ph.D. in Physics, University of California, Riverside, USA (2019–2025) (Advisor: Prof. Chun Hung Lui)
  • B.S. in Physics, University of Science and Technology of China, China (2015–2019)

Awards & Honors

  • Dissertation Completion Fellowship Award (2025)
  • UCR Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award (2024-2025)
  • USTC Outstanding Student Scholarship Silver Award (2016)

Research Interests

  • Interlayer exciton absorption in layered TMDs
  • Interlayer polarizations and sliding ferroelectricity
  • Exciton-polaron effects
  • Moire exciton and charge correlation effects in TMD heterostructures
